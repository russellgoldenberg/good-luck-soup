Shortly after the Japanese bombing of Pearl Harbor in 1942, around over 100,000 Americans of Japanese heritage were sent to internment camps. On February 19th, 1942, President Franklin D. Roosevelt signed Executive Order 9066, beginning the relocation to a dozen camps on the west coast.
